Period Wellness: Sleep, Diet, and Uterus Health

Learn about nourishing your body, understanding period-related conditions, and supporting reproductive health.

Benefits of Good Sleep

  • Mood support: More stable emotions and stress resilience.
  • Energy balance: Better daytime alertness and reduced fatigue.
  • Hormonal rhythm: Consistent routines support natural cycles.
  • Pain tolerance: Rest can help you cope with cramps and discomfort.

Gentle Sleep Habits

  • Wind-down: Dim lights, reduce screens 30â€“60 minutes before bed.
  • Consistency: Regular sleep/wake times anchor your rhythm.
  • Comfort: Cozy bedding and a peaceful room temperature.
  • Breath: Slow breathing or short guided relaxation.

Benefits of a Balanced Diet

  • Steady energy: Mix complex carbs, protein, and healthy fats.
  • Digestion: Fiber and hydration support comfort, especially around your period.
  • Nutrients: Colorful fruits/vegetables, legumes, and whole grains add variety.
  • Mood & sleep: Nourishing meals can support calm and rest.

Gentle Diet Habits

  • Regular meals: Smaller, balanced portions to avoid dips.
  • Hydrate: Water and herbal infusions through the day.
  • Mindful choices: Tune into foods that feel good for your body.
  • Prep: Simple batch-cooking for lower-energy days.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

PCOS is a hormonal disorder that can affect menstrual cycles, ovulation, fertility, and metabolism.

  • Symptoms: Irregular periods, excess hair growth, acne, weight gain.
  • Management: Diet, exercise, and sometimes medication can help regulate cycles.

Polycystic Ovary Disease (PCOD)

PCOD refers to multiple cysts in the ovaries, often causing irregular periods and hormonal imbalance.

  • Symptoms: Menstrual irregularities, weight changes, hair growth, acne.
  • Management: Lifestyle changes, diet, and doctor supervision are recommended.

Endometriosis

Endometriosis occurs when uterine-like tissue grows outside the uterus, causing pain and sometimes fertility issues.

  • Symptoms: Painful periods, heavy bleeding, chronic pelvic pain.
  • Management: Pain management, hormonal therapy, surgery in severe cases.

Periods & Pregnancy

Menstrual cycles change during pregnancy. Missing periods is usually the first sign of pregnancy.

  • Early Signs: Missed period, fatigue, nausea, tender breasts.
  • During Pregnancy: No menstrual bleeding, hormonal support, and regular check-ups are important.
